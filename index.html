Drug Prices in a Free Market

BY LUCY HUANG April 3, 2017
If you take prescription medication on a regular basis, drug pricing can affect how much you spend on medication annually. (Photo: Flick Creative Commons)

While you look for the best price on a car, scour over the internet for the best deal on appliances and shop around for electronics, you should also be doing this for prescription drugs. According to the Reuters business insight report on pharmaceutical pricings, drugs prices in the United States are not regulated by the government. With this free market for drugs, the price of one prescription drug can differ up to hundreds of dollars before your insurance drug benefits kick it.

Why is there such a huge price different?

Pharmacies have benefit managers that negotiate with pharmaceutical companies to get a discount. According to the Pharmaceutical Care Management Association, 80% of the 3.2 billion prescriptions dispensed in American have been negotiated by pharmacy benefit managers. However, retail pharmacy can raise the price of a drug to make a bigger profit.
